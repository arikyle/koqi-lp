## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Real estate agents — experienced, skeptical of industry tools, tired of coaching programs that recycle advice and dashboards that track activity instead of ability. They arrive at this page looking for something that finally measures how good they actually are. Visual and status-driven.

**Secondary**: Brokerage owners — concerned about recruiting and retention, looking for data-driven ways to evaluate and develop agent talent beyond transaction volume.

**Context**: Users land here from word-of-mouth, beta invites, or organic search. They've seen dozens of real estate SaaS pitches. The page must break that pattern in the first viewport.

**Job to be done**: Understand what Koqi is, feel that it's categorically different from existing tools, and request early access — all in a single scroll.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Confident. Precise. Inevitable.

**Voice**: Direct, spare, declarative. Short sentences. Occasional fragments for rhythm. Second person for agents, third person for brokerages.

**Emotional goals**: Quiet authority, intellectual respect, earned trust. The reader should feel seen, not sold to.

**The feel**: "A well-dressed person who doesn't mention their clothes."

**Never**: Exclamation points, superlatives (best, revolutionary, game-changing), buzzwords (leverage, synergy, unlock), emojis, stock photography, agent headshots, handshakes.

**Always**: Precise language, concrete numbers, quiet authority.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Cinematic and confident — closer to a luxury brand film than a SaaS marketing page. Between Aesop and Linear, with one or two moments of genuine visual drama.

**Design bar**: If it could be a Stripe site, we went too restrained. If it could be a crypto launchpad, we went too flashy.

**Theme**: Light mode only. Warm stone background (`#F7F5F1`). Never cold or clinical.

**References**: Aesop (warmth, restraint, brand film quality), Linear (animation quality, developer-grade polish), Kinfolk (editorial photography), Cereal magazine (typography restraint).

**Anti-references**: Keller Williams, Zillow, any typical real estate SaaS marketing. No generic stock photography. No feature grids with icons. No "trusted by" logo bars unless earned.

### Imagery

### Motion

**Dual-library approach:**
- **Framer Motion**: Component-level animation (hero headline stagger, tension word-by-word reveals, section fade-ins)
- **GSAP + ScrollTrigger**: Sticky feature sequence (Section 3 — the centerpiece), ACCS ring animation (Section 5)
- **Lenis**: Smooth scroll

**Motion principles:**
1. Every animation must be defensible — if you can't explain why it exists, cut it
2. No bouncing, no springiness, no overshoot — cubic-bezier easing only
3. Durations: 600ms section reveals, 300ms UI interactions, 1600ms ACCS counter, 800ms headline stagger, 120ms headline line stagger
4. Nothing loops except hero video and product loop
5. Mobile: sticky scroll simplifies to plain fade-ins — sticky is desktop-only
6. `prefers-reduced-motion`: all non-decorative animations disable

### Page Structure

Nine sections, scroll-driven narrative:

1. **Hero** — 100vh, full-bleed video, bottom-left content stack (80px inset), headline stagger 120ms/line with cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1), scroll cue (1px teal line, 48px, opacity pulse 2.4s). Nav: transparent over hero, solid stone after 100vh scroll.
2. **Tension** — 100vh desktop / auto mobile, centered max-w-720px, word-by-word reveal on scroll (30ms stagger via Framer Motion whileInView, once only). Closing line "They track activity. Koqi tracks ability." in Fraunces 40px, 120px top margin.
3. **Features** — 300vh sticky scroll (GSAP ScrollTrigger). Two columns on desktop (50/50), stacked on mobile. Three text/visual pairs cross-fade as user scrolls. Each block = 100vh of scroll. Pin for 300vh then release. **This is the centerpiece.**
4. **Product Glimpse** — 100vh, centered MacBook device frame (1200px max) with video loop. No animation — it's a punctuation mark.
5. **Rewards** — 100vh, ACCS ring (400px diameter, 4px teal stroke, Fraunces 120px center number). Ring fills 0 to 87% over 1.6s, ease-out cubic, on scroll-into-view. Once only.
6. **Brokerage** — 100vh, parallax background at 0.3x scroll speed with 70% stone overlay. The ONLY section with parallax. Outline CTA button (teal border, teal text).
7. **Proof** — 60vh, single testimonial centered, "Now in private beta" small caps label above. No animation.
8. **FAQ** — Auto height, max-w-720px centered accordion. One open at a time, 300ms smooth height transition.
9. **Final CTA** — 80vh, centered, Fraunces 64px, teal fill CTA (56px tall). Quiet ending. Footer: thin 1px divider, `koqi.ai · Built by RealiFi Inc. · hello@koqi.ai` Inter 14px muted.

### Performance Targets

- LCP under 2s
- Hero video under 4MB, H.264 MP4, 1080p max
- Product loop under 2MB
- All images via next/image with proper sizing
- No CLS from sticky sections
- No heavy assets above the fold

### Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: all non-decorative animations disable, content shows statically
- Sufficient contrast ratios on all text/background combinations
- Semantic HTML throughout
- Skip-to-content link
- Alt text on all media

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the design.** Every element must earn its place. When in doubt, remove. White space is not empty — it's the frame that gives the content authority.

2. **Type carries the message.** This is a text-driven page. The copy is the product of careful psychological architecture (pattern interrupt, loss aversion, specificity bias, variable reward). The design must serve the words, not compete with them.

3. **Motion reveals, never decorates.** Animation should feel like the page is breathing — scroll-triggered reveals that guide attention through the narrative. Nothing should move just because it can.

4. **Honesty is the brand.** No mock data. No fabricated numbers. No marketing gloss. If the product screenshot isn't ready, show a single ACCS score card on stone — not a polished fake. The page should feel as rigorous as the product it represents.

5. **The product should feel inevitable, not pitched.** The tone is that of a category-defining platform announcing itself — not a startup begging for signups. Confidence without arrogance. Authority without volume.
